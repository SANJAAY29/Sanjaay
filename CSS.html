VIRAT KOHLI

-the king

Early life:

Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi into a Punjabi Hindu family. His father, Prem Nath Kohli, worked as a criminal lawyer, and his mother, Saroj Kohli, served as a housewife. He has an older brother, Vikas, and an older sister, Bhawna.Kohli's formative years were spent in Uttam Nagar. He commenced his early education at Vishal Bharti Public School.According to his family, Kohli exhibited an early affinity for cricket as a three-year-old. He would pick up a cricket bat and request his father bowl to him.In 1998, the West Delhi Cricket Academy (WDCA) was created. On 30 May of that year, Prem Nath Kohli arranged for his son to meet Rajkumar Sharma.Upon the suggestion of their neighbours, Kohli's father considered enrolling his son in a professional cricket academy, as they believed that his cricketing abilities merited more than just playing in gully cricket.Despite his abilities, he faced the setback of being unable to secure a place in the under-14 Delhi team, supposedly due to extraneous factors. Prem Kohli reportedly received offers to relocate his son to influential clubs, which would have ensured his selection, but he declined the proposals. Kohli eventually found his way into the under-15 Delhi team.He received training at the academy while simultaneously participating in matches at the Sumeet Dogra Academy located at Vasundhara Enclave.In pursuit of furthering his cricketing career, he transitioned to Saviour Convent School during his ninth-grade education. On 18 December 2006, Kohli's father died due to a cerebral attack.According to his mother, Kohli's demeanour shifted noticeably after his father's death. He took cricket with newfound seriousness, prioritizing playing time and dedicating himself fully to the sport.Kohli's family resided in Meera Bagh, Paschim Vihar until the year 2015, after which they relocated to Gurgaon.


Playing Style:

Kohli possesses technical abilities and a dynamic playing demeanour.His customarily batting position in ODl cricket is at number three, with a slightly open-chested stance,and a grip on the bottom handle of his bat. He has been noted for his agile footwork, expansive range of strokes, and his propensity for adeptly orchestrating innings whilst thriving in high-pressure situations.In his batting technique, Kohli tends to favour playing grounded shots rather than attempting big hits. He is appreciated for his ability to execute wrist shots and his consistent prowess in the mid-wicket and cover region.However, Kohli has a vulnerability to deliveries that are wide of the off-stump.Bowlers often exploit this weakness by targeting him with such deliveries, especially in Test and ODl matches.Cricket experts such as VVS Laxman and former India cricketer Sanjay Manjrekar have acknowledged this weakness,and former New Zealand cricketer Richard Hadlee has also noted that Kohli is particularly susceptible to out-swinging deliveries. Kohli has recognition as a limited-overs batsman.In ODIs, he boasts an average of 65.5 runs when batting second and 51.7 runs when batting first.He has amassed 27 of his 50 ODI hundreds in run-chases,and holds the record for the most hundreds scored when batting second.In reference to his success while batting second, Kohli has expressed a fondness for the challenge of chasing, relishing the opportunity to test himself and demonstrate his ability to efficiently manage strike rotation and execute boundary hits.His colleagues admire his self-assurance, dedication, concentration, and work ethic, which are the driving forces behind his success.


International Career:

Virat Kohli's international cricket career is a remarkable journey of consistent performance and leadership, starting from his debut in 2008 to his current status as one of the game's most influential players. Below is a detailed timeline of his career in all formats:

Virat Kohli's international cricket career is a remarkable journey of consistent performance and leadership, starting from his debut in 2008 to his current status as one of the game's most influential players. Below is a detailed timeline of his career in all formats:

1. Debut and Early Years (2008–2011):

ODI Debut: Virat Kohli made his One Day International (ODI) debut for India on August 21, 2008, against Sri Lanka at Dambulla. He came in at No. 3 and scored 12 runs in a losing cause. First International Century: His first ODI century came in 2009, against Sri Lanka in Kolkata, which marked the beginning of his rise in limited-overs cricket. T20I Debut: Kohli made his T20I debut for India in 2010 against Zimbabwe, although his performance was not as striking as in ODIs. 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup: Kohli played a pivotal role in India's 2011 ICC World Cup win. He scored crucial runs, including a key knock of 35 against Australia in the quarter-finals.

2. Rise to Prominence (2011–2014):

Becoming a Regular in the Team: By 2011, Kohli had cemented his place as a regular in India's limited-overs teams due to his consistency and aggressive style of batting. 2013 ICC Champions Trophy: Kohli played a critical role in India's Champions Trophy win in 2013, leading the charge with the bat and contributing significantly in key matches. Test Debut: Kohli made his Test debut on June 20, 2011, against West Indies in Kingston, Jamaica. In his first match, he scored a half-century in his first innings, signaling his ability to adapt to longer formats. First Test Century: His maiden Test century came in 2013 against Australia in Adelaide, helping India to a hard-fought draw in a challenging series.

3. Dominance Across Formats (2014–2017):

2014–2015: Kohli's performances continued to shine, with significant centuries in both ODIs and Tests, including a brilliant 115 in a memorable chase against Australia in the 2016 World Cup. He emerged as a leader in all formats. Captaincy (2013–2021): Kohli was appointed India's vice-captain in 2013, eventually becoming captain of the ODI team in 2013 following MS Dhoni's resignation, and later took over the Test captaincy in 2014 when Dhoni retired from Tests. 2016 T20 World Cup: Kohli starred in the 2016 ICC T20 World Cup, scoring a series of match-winning knocks and earning the Player of the Tournament award. Record-Breaking Years (2016–2017): Kohli began to dominate all formats, breaking numerous records. He became the fastest to 8,000, 9,000, and 10,000 runs in ODIs, setting benchmarks in consistency and run-chases. Test Success: Kohli led India to the No. 1 position in the ICC Test rankings in 2016 and successfully captained India to a historic series win in Australia in 2018–19, a first in India’s history.

4. Continued Excellence and Leadership (2018–2021):

ODI Excellence: Kohli consistently averaged above 50 in ODIs throughout the 2010s, becoming the fastest to 43 centuries, surpassing legendary figures like Ricky Ponting and Sachin Tendulkar. His record in successful chases made him a key figure in India’s 50-over success. 2018–2019: Kohli enjoyed one of his most successful years as a captain. India won series against Sri Lanka, South Africa, and Australia. He also became the first player to score 1,000 runs in T20I cricket in 2019. ICC World Cup 2019: Kohli played a pivotal role in India’s semi-final run, although the team was eliminated by New Zealand. Kohli, however, had an impressive series with the bat, finishing as one of the tournament's top run-scorers. Test Cricket Success: Kohli continued his success in Tests, with standout performances in West Indies, Australia, and South Africa. India, under Kohli's captaincy, achieved series wins in both Australia (2018–19) and the West Indies (2019).

5. Leadership Change and Personal Milestones (2021–Present):

Stepping Down from Captaincy (2021): Kohli stepped down as the captain of India's T20I team after the 2021 ICC T20 World Cup, citing the need to focus on his batting and managing his workload. Later, in 2021, he also resigned from the ODI captaincy following a series defeat in South Africa. Test Captaincy and New Leadership: Kohli remained India's Test captain until early 2022, leading India to numerous successes, including a historic win in Australia. However, after the 2021 South Africa series, he was replaced by Rohit Sharma as the captain for limited-overs cricket. Injuries and Form Challenges (2022–2023): Kohli faced a dip in form during the latter part of 2021 and the early part of 2022, particularly in T20Is and ODIs, leading to questions about his place in the team. However, he made a strong comeback, notably during the 2022 Asia Cup and the 2023 ICC Men's Cricket World Cup, regaining his form and reaching new milestones. Milestones: Kohli became the fastest to 12,000 runs in ODIs, and his landmark 70th international century came in 2022 against Sri Lanka. He has consistently been a key player in India’s campaigns in ICC tournaments, and his longevity and commitment to the sport continue to impress.

6. Key Records and Achievements:

ODI Centuries: Over 46 centuries in ODIs, making him one of the most prolific century-makers in the format. Test Centuries: Kohli has over 27 centuries in Tests, including the record for the most Test centuries by an Indian captain. T20I Performance: Kohli is one of the top run-scorers in T20I cricket, with numerous match-winning knocks. ICC Awards: Kohli has won numerous ICC Player of the Year awards and was named the ICC ODI Player of the Decade for 2010–2020.

Virat Kohli's legacy as a cricketer is cemented not just through his statistical records but also his influence on the game, his consistency across all formats, and his leadership. His career has been characterized by a relentless pursuit of excellence and an immense impact on Indian and world cricket.

IPL Career:

Virat Kohli's IPL career, which began in 2008, has been one of the most significant and enduring in the history of the tournament. Here is a detailed overview of his IPL journey from debut to the present:

1. Early Years and Debut (2008-2010):

Debut: Virat Kohli made his IPL debut in 2008 for the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB), which bought him for ₹12 lakh. He was relatively new to the limelight at that time, having just started his international career. Initial Role: Kohli played as a middle-order batsman in the early seasons. In the 2008 season, he was a promising but inconsistent performer, with a best score of 48 runs. 2009: His performances started improving in 2009, where he played a crucial role in RCB’s run to the finals, though they lost to Deccan Chargers. Kohli’s consistency began to catch the eye of the cricketing world. 2010: In 2010, Kohli’s stock rose further as he became one of the most reliable players for RCB. He scored 246 runs at a healthy average and strike rate, establishing himself as a core member of the team.

2.Establishing Leadership and Breakout Seasons (2011-2013):

2011: By 2011, Kohli had cemented his place as a regular in the top order. He became the vice-captain of RCB, and his leadership skills were already evident. His performances were solid, contributing both with the bat and leadership. 2013: Kohli’s batting skills were on full display as he scored 634 runs in the season, including five fifties, showing his consistency and adaptability in the format. He was beginning to show why he was one of the premier players in T20 cricket.

3.Taking Over the Captaincy (2013-2015):

2013: After the departure of Daniel Vettori, Kohli was made the captain of the Royal Challengers Bangalore in 2013. His leadership skills began to shine, though the team did not manage to win the title in his first season as captain. Kohli's personal form remained strong, with several match-winning innings. 2014-2015: Kohli continued to excel with the bat in these years, despite RCB failing to win the title. His consistency with the bat made him one of the most dangerous players in the tournament, and he regularly scored in the 300-500 run range in these seasons. He began to evolve into a captain capable of leading a team with passion and strategy.

4. Record-Breaking 2016 Season and RCB's Final Appearance:

2016: This was the breakout season of Kohli’s IPL career. He scored an astounding 973 runs, including four centuries, in 16 matches, breaking multiple records. His tally of runs remains the highest-ever in a single IPL season, and his form was unmatched, showcasing his dominance across all formats of the game. He led RCB to the final of the tournament, though they fell short to Sunrisers Hyderabad in the final. 2016 IPL: Kohli’s IPL season was historic for individual achievements—he not only set the record for most runs in a season but also finished with a remarkable average of 81.08 and a strike rate of 152.03. He was awarded the Player of the Tournament for this sensational season.

5. Continued Consistency but Unsuccessful Campaigns (2017-2020):

2017: Kohli’s consistency continued as he scored 308 runs, but RCB had a disappointing season and finished outside the playoff spots. Despite his individual brilliance, the team could not find the winning formula. 2018-2020: In these seasons, Kohli continued to score heavily, but RCB struggled to make it to the playoffs. His leadership was never in doubt, but RCB's inability to perform as a team meant that they often finished mid-table or lower. In these years, Kohli's individual performances remained one of the few bright spots for RCB, but he was unable to lead them to a title or even a top-four finish.

6. Leadership Change and New Beginnings (2021-Present):

2021: Kohli announced at the start of the season that he would step down as captain after the 2021 IPL season, marking the end of an era of leadership for RCB. Under his leadership, the team had not managed to win the IPL title, but he remained one of the tournament's top players. In 2021, Kohli’s personal form dipped slightly, and RCB did not make it to the finals again. He scored 405 runs in the season at an average of 28.92. Post-Captaincy: After stepping down as captain, RCB appointed Faf du Plessis as the new captain ahead of the 2022 season. Kohli continued as a key player for the franchise, although the focus was more on him getting back to his peak form. 2022-2023: Kohli struggled with his form in 2022, but after the IPL 2022 season, he had a resurgence in international cricket, which also helped him regain his confidence in the IPL. He managed to put up decent performances in 2023, with a return to his vintage batting style.

Key Stats and Achievements in IPL:

Runs: As of 2024, Kohli has scored over 6,500 runs in IPL, making him one of the highest run-scorers in the history of the tournament. Centuries: Kohli has scored 7 centuries in the IPL, with a high score of 122*. Highest Runs in a Season: His 973 runs in 2016 remain the highest-ever total in a single IPL season. Captaining RCB: Under his leadership, RCB finished as runners-up in 2009 and 2016. Despite his leadership, RCB has never won the IPL title, making it a bittersweet part of his IPL journey. Milestones: Kohli has been one of the most consistent performers in IPL history, accumulating multiple 500+ run seasons, earning him a reputation as one of the best T20 players in the world.

Virat Kohli’s IPL career has been marked by individual brilliance, leadership, and an enduring presence at the top. Despite not winning the IPL title with RCB, Kohli has been an integral figure in the tournament, consistently delivering world-class performances. His record-breaking 2016 season is one of the most memorable campaigns in IPL history, and his dedication to RCB has made him a fan favorite. Even after stepping down as captain, Kohli’s contributions continue to be crucial for the franchise.

Personal Life:

Kohli's romantic association with Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma, which commenced in 2013, earned the duo the moniker of "Virushka".During an interview with Graham Bensinger, Kohli divulged that he had encountered Sharma for the first time, when they were both were engaged in a promotional shoot for Clear shampoo.Their union since then has attracted significant media interest.On 11 December 2017, the couple exchanged nuptials in a ceremony held in Florence, Italy.On 11 January 2021, the couple had their first child, a daughter, named Vamika.On 15 February 2024, the couple had a second child, a boy named Akaay. In 2018, Kohli disclosed that he had made the decision to adopt a vegetarian diet in an effort to alleviate the symptoms of a cervical spine issue caused by elevated levels of uric acid. This condition was impacting his finger movements, and thus, affecting his performance as a batsman. He made a conscious effort to abstain from consuming meat, as part of his regimen for maintaining optimal health.He has since clarified that his dietary choices do not align with a vegan lifestyle and he continues to consume dairy products and eggs effectively making what is known in India as an eggetarian.His hard work and discipline have earned him the reputation of being one of the fittest cricketers in the world. Kohli has acknowledged a belief in superstitions and owns various lucky charms and rituals that he feels serve to bring him good fortune on the cricket field. One such tradition involves the donning of black wristband. Additionally, he prefers a particular pair of gloves, which he has consistently worn.Furthermore, Kohli has been observed sporting a kara, a traditional bangle often worn for religious or spiritual purposes, on his right arm since 2012.In addition to the previously mentioned superstitions, Kohli has also established the ritual of consistently donning white shoes on the cricket field.Kohli has tattoos of the Hindu deity Lord Shiva, the sacred syllable "Om", the names of his parents, Prem and Saroj, a tribal emblem, a representation of a serene monastery, a samurai warrior, the astrological symbol of the scorpion, and his ODl and Test match cap numbers.

Outside Cricket:

Kohli invested in Indian Super League club FC Goa, which he co-owned as of 2024. Beyond the realm of cricket, this venture served as a future business opportunity for him, as he sought to diversify his portfolio after retirement from the sport. In November 2014, Kohli partnered with Anjana Reddy's Universal Sportsbiz (USPL) to launch the youth fashion brand WROGN. The brand specialized in men's casual wear clothing and entered into collaborations with retail outlets such as Myntra and Shopper's Stop.Additionally, in late 2014, Kohli became a shareholder and brand ambassador for the London-based social networking venture "Sport Convo". His involvement aimed to promote and raise awareness of the platform, which was focused on providing a space for sports fans to connect and engage with one another. In 2015, Kohli invested a sum of ₹90 crore (US$11 million) towards the establishment of a chain of health clubs and fitness centers throughout India. Dubbed as "Chisel", the network of gyms was established through a joint venture between Kohli, Chisel India, and CSE (Cornerstone Sport and Entertainment), the organization responsible for managing Kohli's commercial pursuits.In September of the same year, Kohli expanded his portfolio of sports-related investments by becoming a co-owner of the International Premier Tennis League franchise, UAE Royals.In quick succession, he also became a co-owner of the JSW-owned Bengaluru Yodhas franchise in the Pro Wrestling League. In the year 2016, Kohli joined forces with Stepathlon Lifestyle to launch Stepathlon Kids. The initiative aimed to encourage young individuals to adopt healthy lifestyle habits and inculcate the love for physical activity. In 2017, Kohli entered into a partnership with the German sportswear brand, Puma, to launch his athletic lifestyle brand, One8. This brand offers a range of sport-related apparel, footwear, and accessories.That same year, Kohli also established Nueva, a fine dining establishment located in New Delhi. The restaurant features a South American-inspired decor accentuated with Native American artwork, providing a distinctive culinary setting.Kohli also established One8 Commune, a chain of restaurant-bars. This marked his inaugural foray into the food and beverage industry. The first outlet of this restaurant was launched in 2017. In 2022, Kohli and his spouse made an investment of ₹2.5 crore (equivalent to ₹2.9 crore or US$350,000 in 2023) in Digit, an insurance-based startup.Additionally, they also extended their support to Blue Tribe, a startup that specializes in the production and distribution of plant-based meat products. Driven by his own inclination towards a plant-based diet, Kohli aims to raise awareness and encourage individuals to adopt more sustainable and environmentally conscious practices.
